# Own Your Substrate

> **Serious operators in AI are not renting the layer beneath their work. They are buying it, building it, or keeping it on their own disk.** The shape of the "substrate" differs — domain talent, production capability, local files — but the instinct is the same across every ingested source.

## The three instances

| Source | The layer being owned | Why it can't be rented |
|---|---|---|
| **[anthropic](../entities/anthropic.md) buying [coefficient-bio](../entities/coefficient-bio.md)** (~$400M) | First-party [ai-drug-discovery](../concepts/ai-drug-discovery.md) expertise from ex-[prescient-design](../entities/prescient-design.md) team | Domain tacit knowledge compounds in people, not APIs. Acquisition followed, not preceded, the [claude-for-life-sciences](../entities/claude-for-life-sciences.md) launch — they already had the product |
| **Indian studios standing up in-house AI labs** | [collective-artists-network](../entities/collective-artists-network.md)/Galleri5 mocap+AI pipeline; [abundantia-entertainment](../entities/abundantia-entertainment.md)'s $11M build; [eros-media-world](../entities/eros-media-world.md)'s 3,000-title catalog review | Production craft compounds in pipelines tuned by repetition. Google/Microsoft/Nvidia partnerships exist — studios treat them as commodity compute, not as the capability itself |
| **Karpathy's local Markdown files** | [llm-knowledge-bases](../concepts/llm-knowledge-bases.md) on disk, LLM as librarian, no SaaS vendor | Research context compounds in auditable structured artifacts. When a SaaS vendor changes terms, the compounding stops. [file-over-app-philosophy](../concepts/file-over-app-philosophy.md) is load-bearing |

## Why the same instinct keeps appearing

Three surface-different stories, one underlying logic: **the parts of the AI stack that compound over time are the parts you cannot rent.**

- Domain knowledge compounds in people and institutions.
- Production craft compounds in pipelines and muscle memory.
- Research context compounds in structured, auditable artifacts.

In every case the strategic move is the same: **identify the layer where your compounding advantage lives, and refuse to let anyone else own it.** Rent the commodity layers below (frontier APIs, cloud compute, distribution platforms). Own the layer above.

## What this predicts

- **More vertical acqui-hires from frontier labs.** Expect similar moves in materials science, climate, law — and OpenAI / Google DeepMind responding in kind.
- **More in-house AI studios at content companies.** Game studios, ad agencies, and publishers are natural next sites for the Indian-film model (commodity compute + proprietary pipelines).
- **Growing markets for file-over-app infrastructure.** Tooling that treats local Markdown corpora as a first-class substrate — search, indexing, multi-agent coordination, [contamination-mitigation](../concepts/contamination-mitigation.md)-style clean/messy vault separation. The VentureBeat piece quoted multiple enterprise voices explicitly saying *"we're building this."*
- **A widening gap between operators and tool users.** Renters look productive in the short term and structurally disadvantaged in the long term.

## Where the pattern doesn't apply

- **True commodity use cases.** One-off reports, throwaway scripts — rent top-to-bottom. [ephemeral-wiki](../concepts/ephemeral-wiki.md) implicitly concedes this.
- **Small operators without compounding advantage to defend.** The instinct only pays off if there's something that actually compounds in the owning.
- **Regulatory environments that forbid it.** The Hollywood flip side of the India divergence: SAG-AFTRA and DGA contracts legally prevent US studios from owning certain parts of the stack. When law blocks ownership, the operator's only option is to rent — or move jurisdictions.

## Where this wiki sits in the pattern

**This wiki is itself an instance of the pattern it describes.** The project runs on local Markdown files, uses the LLM as a librarian rather than a SaaS substrate, and is designed to compound in auditable human-readable form. Either elegant or circular depending on your temperament; noted explicitly.
