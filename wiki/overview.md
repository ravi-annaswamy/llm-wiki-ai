# Overview

High-level synthesis of everything in the wiki. Updated as sources are ingested.

## Current State

Nine sources ingested, spanning two vertical-AI application stories, one knowledge-management methodology source, two automated-research methodology sources, one creative-writing / authorship essay, one long-horizon-agentic-coding methodology source, one foundational ML theory reference (score-based / diffusion generative modeling), and one content-provenance / authenticity-labelling opinion piece:

1. **Life sciences** — [anthropic](entities/anthropic.md)'s April 2026 acquisition of [coefficient-bio](entities/coefficient-bio.md) for a reported $400M, following the October 2025 launch of [claude-for-life-sciences](entities/claude-for-life-sciences.md). A frontier AI lab buying ex-[prescient-design](entities/prescient-design.md) drug-discovery talent to build first-party domain expertise in [ai-drug-discovery](concepts/ai-drug-discovery.md).
2. **Indian film industry** — A Reuters investigation documenting India's unprecedented-scale deployment of AI in filmmaking ([ai-filmmaking-india](concepts/ai-filmmaking-india.md)). Studios like [collective-artists-network](entities/collective-artists-network.md) (Galleri5), [eros-media-world](entities/eros-media-world.md), [jiostar](entities/jiostar.md), [abundantia-entertainment](entities/abundantia-entertainment.md), and [neuralgarage](entities/neuralgarage.md) are pursuing three distinct plays: fully AI-generated mythology content, [ai-dubbing](concepts/ai-dubbing.md) across 22 languages, and [ai-film-re-editing](concepts/ai-film-re-editing.md) of catalog titles. Cost claims: 1/5 traditional production cost, 1/4 traditional timeline (per [rahul-regulapati](entities/rahul-regulapati.md)).
3. **LLM Knowledge Bases methodology** (meta) — A VentureBeat writeup of [andrej-karpathy](entities/andrej-karpathy.md)'s "LLM Knowledge Bases" pattern: treat the LLM as a full-time research librarian that compiles, lints, and backlinks a Markdown wiki from raw sources. This is the pattern **this wiki itself implements** — see [llm-knowledge-bases](concepts/llm-knowledge-bases.md). Community extensions: [contamination-mitigation](concepts/contamination-mitigation.md) ([steph-ango](entities/steph-ango.md)), [swarm-knowledge-base](concepts/swarm-knowledge-base.md), [ephemeral-wiki](concepts/ephemeral-wiki.md).
4. **Automated algorithm discovery (MARL / game theory)** — A MarkTechPost writeup of a [google-deepmind](entities/google-deepmind.md) paper applying [alphaevolve](entities/alphaevolve.md) to [counterfactual-regret-minimization](concepts/counterfactual-regret-minimization.md) and [policy-space-response-oracles](concepts/policy-space-response-oracles.md) in [marl-imperfect-information-games](concepts/marl-imperfect-information-games.md). Uses Gemini 2.5 Pro as the **mutation operator over actual Python source code**, not over hyperparameters. Discovered VAD-CFR (matches SoTA in 10/11 games) and SHOR-PSRO (8/11), including genuinely non-intuitive mechanisms like a hard warm-start at iteration 500 and asymmetric training/evaluation solver configurations. See [llm-driven-algorithm-discovery](concepts/llm-driven-algorithm-discovery.md).
5. **AI in creative writing** — A New Yorker essay ("Is It Wrong to Write a Book with AI?") framing the authorship debate around two poles: [shy-girl-ai-novel](entities/shy-girl-ai-novel.md) (a Hachette horror novel unmasked by [pangram](entities/pangram.md) at 78% AI-generated, cancelled, 4/5 Amazon rating) and [coral-hart](entities/coral-hart.md) (a pseudonymous romance novelist running an industrial [ai-novel-factory](concepts/ai-novel-factory.md) at six figures). Introduces the [drum-machine-analogy-for-ai](concepts/drum-machine-analogy-for-ai.md) (TR-808 in 1983 as a template for where AI writing sits in 2026) and the [layered-authorship](concepts/layered-authorship.md) argument (fiction is separable into premise/plot/prose/voice, and different layers can be distributed across contributors — AI included). See [ai-in-creative-writing](concepts/ai-in-creative-writing.md) as the hub.
7. **Foundational ML theory — score-based generative models** — [yang-song](entities/yang-song.md)'s widely cited blog post compiling his 2019–2022 paper sequence on [score-based-generative-models](concepts/score-based-generative-models.md). Parameterize the score function ∇ₓ log p(x) instead of the density, train with [score-matching](concepts/score-matching.md) (denoising variant in practice), sample with [langevin-dynamics](concepts/langevin-dynamics.md) (annealed across multiple noise scales to fix high-dimensional failure). Generalizing to infinite noise scales yields a stochastic-differential-equation framework that **unifies score-based modeling with [diffusion-models](concepts/diffusion-models.md)** under one continuous-time process — Song's own analogy is wave mechanics vs matrix mechanics. The [probability-flow-ode](concepts/probability-flow-ode.md) turns the same trained model into a continuous normalizing flow for exact log-likelihoods, and Bayes' rule for scores lets a single unconditional model solve arbitrary inverse problems (inpainting, colorization, MRI, CT) at test time without retraining. This is the first foundational-ML-theory source in the wiki and a distinct content cluster from the applied / agentic work that dominates the rest.

6. **Long-running agentic coding for scientific computing** — An Anthropic post by [siddharth-mishra-sharma](entities/siddharth-mishra-sharma.md) (Discovery team) describing how to apply multi-day autonomous agentic coding workflows to scientific computing, even outside one's domain. Concrete walkthrough: [clax-project](entities/clax-project.md), a differentiable JAX Boltzmann solver built by Claude Opus 4.6 in days, reaching sub-percent accuracy vs the [class-boltzmann-solver](entities/class-boltzmann-solver.md) C reference — where domain-expert teams typically invest months to years. Names the scaffolding pieces: a `CLAUDE.md` portable plan, a `CHANGELOG.md` cross-session memory ([agent-persistent-memory](concepts/agent-persistent-memory.md)), a [test-oracle-for-agents](concepts/test-oracle-for-agents.md), git as coordination, tmux-on-SLURM execution, and the [ralph-loop](concepts/ralph-loop.md) to counter [agentic-laziness](concepts/agentic-laziness.md). See [long-running-agentic-coding](concepts/long-running-agentic-coding.md) (the general pattern) and [agent-driven-scientific-computing](concepts/agent-driven-scientific-computing.md) (the domain-specific case).

9. **Content provenance and human-made labelling** — A Verge opinion piece by Jess Weatherbed (2026-04-04) surveying the fragmented landscape of [human-made-content-labelling](concepts/human-made-content-labelling.md) schemes (12+ competing entrants: [proudly-human](entities/proudly-human.md), [not-by-ai](entities/not-by-ai.md), [proof-i-did-it](entities/proof-i-did-it.md), Made by Human, No-AI-Icon, Authors Guild HAC, …) against the backdrop of the established but "wholly ineffectual" AI-labelling standard [c2pa](entities/c2pa.md). The structural argument, borrowed from Instagram's Adam Mosseri, is [fingerprinting-real-vs-fake](concepts/fingerprinting-real-vs-fake.md): label the side whose incentives are aligned with cooperation. The piece catalogs four verification approaches (trust, AI-detection, manual audit, threshold-voluntary, blockchain attestation), the definition problem ("does chatting with an LLM about the idea count?"), and the structural ceilings (fragmentation, no regulator, post-hoc-only enforcement). It also surfaces a cross-source discrepancy on [coral-hart](entities/coral-hart.md)'s disclosure practice — The New Yorker says "sometimes does and sometimes doesn't," The Verge says "doesn't have a label on any of her books" — both citing the same Alter/NYT reporting. Flagged on the coral-hart page.

8. **Karpathy's AutoResearch** — A DataCamp tutorial walking through [andrej-karpathy](entities/andrej-karpathy.md)'s [autoresearch](entities/autoresearch.md) project (released 2026-03-07, 21,000+ GitHub stars within days). An open-source three-file contract that lets an LLM coding agent run ML experiments overnight on a single GPU, keeping only changes that improve validation loss. `prepare.py` (immutable evaluator) + `train.py` (agent sandbox) + `program.md` (human-authored directives) + `"NEVER STOP"` directive + 5-minute fixed budget per experiment. The filter is a strict [ratchet-loop](concepts/ratchet-loop.md): every experiment is either committed or `git reset HEAD~1`-reverted based on `val_bpb`. Results: initial overnight run 83 experiments / 15 kept / `val_bpb` 1.000 → 0.975; production impact on Karpathy's `nanochat` 11% faster time-to-GPT-2; Shopify's Tobi Lutke got 19% validation improvement from 37 experiments on a 0.8B-param query-expansion model day one. Extends Karpathy's methodological arc from [llm-knowledge-bases](concepts/llm-knowledge-bases.md) (knowledge management) to automated experimentation; both are [producer-filter-pattern](analyses/producer-filter-pattern.md) instances with Markdown-based human direction and mechanical filters. Also ingested are [agentic-engineering](concepts/agentic-engineering.md) (Karpathy's three-stage vibe-coding → agentic-engineering → independent-research progression) and the [llm-research-creativity-ceiling](concepts/llm-research-creativity-ceiling.md) that AutoResearch exposes (ratchet can't traverse valleys; RLHF makes the agent "cagy and scared" on open-ended moves).

## Key Themes

- **Vertical AI strategy over horizontal API access.** In the application stories, operators are building (or buying) first-party domain capability — Anthropic acquiring a drug-discovery team, Indian studios standing up in-house AI labs rather than just licensing tools. The Karpathy pattern is the *knowledge-management* expression of the same instinct: own your structured context, don't rent it from a SaaS.
- **Regulatory and contractual environment shapes where the frontier lives.** The India/Hollywood divergence is explicit: SAG-AFTRA and DGA contracts constrain US studios from the exact practices that Indian studios are deploying freely. Frontiers move to where friction is lowest.
- **Structure over similarity for mid-sized, high-signal corpora.** The Karpathy pattern's bet is that the industry has over-indexed on vector DBs/RAG for problems that are fundamentally about *structure* (backlinks, indices, explicit connections) rather than *similarity*. See [rag-vs-wiki-comparison](concepts/rag-vs-wiki-comparison.md).
- **The economic case is sharpest where stylization hides AI limits.** Indian mythology works as the AI beachhead genre precisely because stylized visuals mask current generative-video weaknesses. Contemporary drama and live-action remain harder.
- **Talent lineage matters.** Prescient Design (Genentech) is emerging as a recognizable origin point for AI drug-discovery founders. Expect similar patterns in other verticals.
- **Commercial success and critical reception are decoupling (now 3 instances).** The AI Mahabharat series has a 1.4/10 IMDb rating but 26.5M+ views. The Raanjhanaa AI re-release drew public backlash from its lead actor but sold 35% of Tamil tickets in release month (12 points above average). The Shy Girl AI novel was cancelled by Hachette after a 78% AI-detection finding but had already earned a 4/5 Amazon rating from readers who didn't know. The same pattern now appears across two media (film, fiction) and two cultural contexts (Indian mythology TV, American self-publishing). The Hachette cancellation is a hint that the industry believes the ratings wouldn't survive mandatory disclosure — but the ratings themselves are evidence that audience behavior, absent disclosure, does not track critical signals.
- **File-over-app and data sovereignty as an emerging AI posture.** Karpathy, [steph-ango](entities/steph-ango.md), and the enterprise voices in the VentureBeat piece all converge on a picture where the *data* lives on the local disk as open files, and AI models visit it as agents. This is in direct tension with the SaaS-heavy knowledge-store model (Notion, Google Docs).
- **LLM as active producer of structured artifacts, not just query-answerer.** Five clean instances across the wiki's sources: Karpathy's lint-filtered [llm-knowledge-bases](concepts/llm-knowledge-bases.md), AlphaEvolve's exploitability-filtered [llm-driven-algorithm-discovery](concepts/llm-driven-algorithm-discovery.md), Coral Hart's revision-filtered [ai-novel-factory](concepts/ai-novel-factory.md), CLAX's accuracy-filtered [long-running-agentic-coding](concepts/long-running-agentic-coding.md), and now Karpathy's `val_bpb`-filtered [autoresearch](entities/autoresearch.md). Plus a sixth plausible instance in Coefficient Bio's implied drug-discovery pipeline. In every case the LLM generates a durable output and a mechanical filter loop decides what survives. **Graduated to its own cross-cutting analysis page during the 2026-04-04 refactor: see [producer-filter-pattern](analyses/producer-filter-pattern.md).** AutoResearch is the first instance in the wiki with a **literally uncircumventable** filter (immutable `prepare.py` the producer has no write access to) and a **strictly monotone** admissibility rule ([ratchet-loop](concepts/ratchet-loop.md)).
- **Karpathy's methodological arc is now a recognizable two-source cluster.** Both Karpathy sources in the wiki describe the same architecture — human sets direction in Markdown, LLM produces durable artifacts on disk, mechanical filter decides what survives — applied to different substrates. The [llm-knowledge-bases](concepts/llm-knowledge-bases.md) source uses Markdown wiki pages filtered by lint passes; [autoresearch](entities/autoresearch.md) uses git commits to `train.py` filtered by `val_bpb`. Together they sketch [agentic-engineering](concepts/agentic-engineering.md) as a progression from vibe coding (human at the keyboard) to agentic engineering (human orchestrating agents) to independent research (human writing a `program.md` and walking away). The compounding work in all three stages lives in the **artifact repository plus filter**, not in the model weights or the chat UI.
- **Ratchet loops trade exploration for audit trails and uncircumventability.** The [ratchet-loop](concepts/ratchet-loop.md) concept names the strict sub-variant of the producer–filter pattern where every change is either committed or reverted against a fixed, cheap-to-run evaluator. It is what lets AutoResearch run overnight without supervision on a single GPU — but it cannot traverse a metric valley, which is the root of the [llm-research-creativity-ceiling](concepts/llm-research-creativity-ceiling.md). Contrast with population-based evolutionary filters like [alphaevolve](entities/alphaevolve.md), which tolerate temporarily-dominated candidates and therefore find genuinely novel structures (hard warm-starts at iteration 500, asymmetric train/eval solver configs) but need DeepMind-scale compute per generation. The design space is not "evolution yes/no" — it's "how tight is the scaffolding, how expensive is one evaluation, how often can you close the loop."
- **Task shape determines agent topology.** A new observation from the Mishra-Sharma post: the same long-horizon autonomous agentic-coding methodology plays out in two topologically opposite ways depending on the task. Anthropic's [anthropic-c-compiler-project](entities/anthropic-c-compiler-project.md) parallelized across ~2,000 mostly independent sessions; Mishra-Sharma's [clax-project](entities/clax-project.md) used a **single sequential agent** spawning subagents as needed, because a deeply coupled numerical pipeline requires causal tracing through the whole chain. This generalizes: the swarm-vs-solo choice isn't about the methodology, it's about whether the artifact decomposes naturally or is internally coupled. Expect this to matter for future automated-research / agent-driven-scientific-computing work.
- **CLAUDE.md + CHANGELOG.md as a generalizable "agent context pair."** Two files — a portable plan the agent can edit, and a progress/failure log the agent reads at session start — together do the work of a hidden vector-store memory, in a form that is legible, portable, and editable by the user. This is a [file-over-app-philosophy](concepts/file-over-app-philosophy.md) instance and a structural cousin of this wiki's own `CLAUDE.md` + `wiki/log.md` split. The pattern is likely applicable beyond code — writing, research notebooks, multi-day analysis pipelines — wherever the work has memory-length problems and a filter loop.
- **The substrate beneath the applied visual-AI stories is now explicit.** The text-to-video, image-to-image, voice-cloning, and facial-alteration systems referenced throughout [ai-filmmaking-india](concepts/ai-filmmaking-india.md), [ai-dubbing](concepts/ai-dubbing.md), [hindu-mythology-ai-genre](concepts/hindu-mythology-ai-genre.md), and implicitly in [ai-novel-factory](concepts/ai-novel-factory.md) are all descendants of the score-based / diffusion generative-model family established by [yang-song](entities/yang-song.md), [stefano-ermon](entities/stefano-ermon.md), and collaborators between 2019 and 2021. Ingesting Song's tutorial ([2026-04-04-yang-song-score-based-generative-modeling](sources/2026-04-04-yang-song-score-based-generative-modeling.md)) is the wiki's first foundational-theory source and makes the "own your substrate" split concrete: Indian studios, AI novel factories, and dubbing shops rent the diffusion-model layer (Veo, SDXL, open-source checkpoints, proprietary voice models) and own the domain pipelines, catalogs, and distribution wrapped around it. Cross-link added from the three applied visual-AI concept pages.
- **Provenance as an incentive-alignment problem, not a technical one.** The Verge source on [human-made-content-labelling](concepts/human-made-content-labelling.md) reframes the AI-authenticity debate around [fingerprinting-real-vs-fake](concepts/fingerprinting-real-vs-fake.md): the reason [c2pa](entities/c2pa.md) has broad corporate commitment yet remains "wholly ineffectual" is that its cooperative party is the wrong one. AI producers (influencers, synthetic-porn clones, [coral-hart](entities/coral-hart.md)-style novel factories, Etsy scammers) lose revenue when labelled, so they don't participate. Human creators, by contrast, are motivated to distinguish their work, which is why 12+ human-made labelling schemes have sprung up in the last two years despite the absence of regulatory backing. This is a sibling pattern to [own-your-substrate](analyses/own-your-substrate.md) at the trust-infrastructure level: the identity layer that compounds for a creator ([proof-i-did-it](entities/proof-i-did-it.md)-style on-chain history) is distinct from the per-file metadata layer (C2PA) that platforms can strip in transit. The wiki now has its first source focused on provenance as an independent axis from quality, economics, or capability.
- **Authorship as layered, not unitary.** The New Yorker essay crystallizes an argument this wiki was already half-making through [ai-film-re-editing](concepts/ai-film-re-editing.md) and [ai-dubbing](concepts/ai-dubbing.md): creative works are layered (premise, plot, scene, dialogue, prose, voice for fiction; performance, voice, facial motion, ending for film), and AI interventions are always at **specific layers**, not wholesale. The controversy tracks which layer is being touched. Prose and voice are the highest-resistance layers in fiction; actor voice identity is the analogous high-resistance layer in film. See [layered-authorship](concepts/layered-authorship.md).

## Key Figures to Watch

- The EY projection: AI could boost Indian media/entertainment revenue by 10% and cut costs by 15% over the medium term.
- Galleri5's 1/5 cost and 1/4 time figures for mythology/fantasy.
- Abundantia's target: one-third of revenue from AI-generated or AI-assisted content within three years.
- Eros's 3,000-title catalog review for AI re-editing candidates.
- Karpathy's scale claim for LLM Knowledge Bases: ~100 articles / ~400,000 words is comfortably navigable without "fancy RAG."
- AlphaEvolve benchmark result: VAD-CFR matches or surpasses state-of-the-art in 10 of 11 games; SHOR-PSRO in 8 of 11, with no re-tuning between training and test games.
- CLAX result: sub-percent accuracy against CLASS on main CMB angular power spectra, reached in days of Claude Opus 4.6 wall time on a single H100 node — vs. months-to-years for domain-expert groups building comparable differentiable JAX Boltzmann solvers.
- Accuracy target chosen for CLAX: 0.1%, matching the typical CLASS↔CAMB agreement level (i.e., the field's floor for "correct").
- Anthropic C compiler project scale: ~2,000 sessions to build a Linux-kernel-capable compiler.
- AutoResearch initial overnight run: 83 experiments, 15 improvements kept, `val_bpb` 1.000 → 0.975, on a single GPU.
- AutoResearch production impact on Karpathy's `nanochat`: time-to-GPT-2 benchmark from 2.02h → 1.80h (11% faster).
- Shopify (Tobi Lutke) AutoResearch adaptation: 19% validation improvement from 37 experiments on a 0.8B-param query-expansion model, reported day after he started.
- AutoResearch per-experiment budget: 5-minute fixed wall-clock, ~12 experiments/hour, ~80–100 per overnight run.
- Karpathy's production AutoResearch setup: 8×H100 "bigger cousin" running against `nanochat`.

## Open Questions

- Is Anthropic planning a series of vertical acqui-hires in other scientific domains, or is life sciences a uniquely high-priority bet?
- How far does the Indian mythology-genre AI tolerance transfer to other genres?
- Will Hollywood union constraints erode as Indian economics are demonstrated, or will the two industries permanently diverge?
- What does "AI cost savings" look like outside mythology and fantasy?
- Do competitors (OpenAI, Google DeepMind, biotech-AI pure plays) respond with similar vertical acquisitions?
- At what corpus size does the Karpathy pattern break? Is unsupervised linting reliable without a human editor?
- Does this wiki itself need to adopt [contamination-mitigation](concepts/contamination-mitigation.md) (a staging directory) before multi-source batch ingest becomes safe?
- How far does [llm-driven-algorithm-discovery](concepts/llm-driven-algorithm-discovery.md) transfer outside game theory? Optimizers, attention variants, compiler passes, RL algorithms are all plausible targets — does the approach need tight class hierarchies, or does it work over looser interfaces?
- How much of AlphaEvolve's "discovery" is genuine novelty vs. the LLM retrieving and recombining tricks from its training data?
- Is the reception paradox (Mahabharat / Raanjhanaa / Shy Girl) conditional on non-disclosure, or robust to it? The Hachette cancellation suggests the industry thinks non-disclosure; the Mahabharat numbers suggest disclosure doesn't matter when the genre tolerance is high enough.
- Does the [ai-novel-factory](concepts/ai-novel-factory.md) model generalize beyond romance and other formulaic genres, or is it structurally capped at the mid-market?
- In a disclosure regime, would readers want to know *which layers* of a book were AI-assisted (outline only? prose? voice?), or just whether AI was involved at all?
- How far does [long-running-agentic-coding](concepts/long-running-agentic-coding.md) generalize outside scientific computing and compiler-building? Which task shapes fit; which don't? What is the failure mode at the multi-week or multi-month horizon (CLAX is days; the compiler is longer but parallel)?
- Does the "non-expert-driven agent-built scientific code" result in [clax-project](entities/clax-project.md) replicate in other domains? Where does domain-literacy become the binding constraint vs. scaffolding skill?
- When does [agentic-laziness](concepts/agentic-laziness.md) disappear purely from model capability improvements, making scaffolds like the [ralph-loop](concepts/ralph-loop.md) obsolete?
- (Resolved 2026-04-04 refactor) The "test oracle / filter loop" theme was promoted to its own page: [producer-filter-pattern](analyses/producer-filter-pattern.md). Open follow-on: does a sixth substrate (DFT / materials, theorem proving, legal drafting) appear in the next several ingests that extends the pattern further?
- Does the AutoResearch [ratchet-loop](concepts/ratchet-loop.md) generalize outside ML training? Compilers, search ranking, query expansion, and numerical solvers all satisfy the structural requirements (cheap immutable evaluator, fixed per-experiment budget), but only query expansion has a reported instance so far (Shopify/Lutke). Where does the pattern fail?
- Is the [llm-research-creativity-ceiling](concepts/llm-research-creativity-ceiling.md) primarily in the ratchet filter or in RLHF-trained producer conservatism? The two sources interact, which makes it hard to separate empirically. A non-RLHF producer running an identical ratchet would be an informative experiment.
- Does a minimal modification to AutoResearch — e.g., a "speculative branch" mode permitting one multi-step detour per N experiments — preserve its audit trail while relaxing the creativity ceiling? Or does any relaxation at all require moving to a population-based filter?
