# Layered Authorship

> **Fiction is not a single undifferentiated act of creation but a layered endeavor — premise, plot, character, scene, dialogue, prose, voice — in which different layers can be produced by different contributors without the result ceasing to "count" as fiction.** The New Yorker essay's structuring move: *"must all the layers be made by the same individual?"* Empirical answer across the culture is already "no" — we just rarely make it explicit.

## Pre-AI forms of layered authorship

| Form | How layering works |
|---|---|
| Journalism | Writers + editors, assumed |
| Prestige TV | Writers' rooms, "showrunner" as author |
| Screenwriting | Oscar "Original" = *not an adaptation*, not *one author*; uncredited doctors are routine |
| James Patterson | Detailed outlines to collaborators; ~15 books/year; ~30 simultaneous projects; 1/17 US hardcover novels |
| Franchise continuation | Alex Cross since 1993 — a factory is simply required |

## The implicit contract with the reader

Our expectations about authorship **vary silently by context** and are rarely articulated. Booker winners assumed to have written every word; journalists assumed to have had editors; TV shows assumed to have writers' rooms; Patterson thrillers assumed to be brand more than prose. Readers don't notice because they're never forced to. **AI breaks the silence** — makes the contract negotiable, which means it has to be renegotiated explicitly. That renegotiation is happening in public through [shy-girl-ai-novel](../entities/shy-girl-ai-novel.md) and [coral-hart](../entities/coral-hart.md).

## The resistance gradient by layer

| Layer | Human collaboration tolerated | AI resistance |
|---|---|---|
| Premise | Routinely (editors, pitches) | Uncontroversial |
| Plot / outline | Routinely (Patterson, writers' rooms) | Low |
| Scene construction | Sometimes (co-authors, ghostwriters) | Medium |
| Dialogue | Sometimes (script doctors) | Medium |
| **Prose / style** | Rarely outside journalism | **High — where Shy Girl was caught** |
| Voice | Almost never | Highest |

**Almost all current controversy sits at the prose/voice layer.** If AI contributes to premise or outline, readers object less. Shy Girl was detectable because AI was used at the *prose* layer — the layer readers have always expected to be the author's own.

## The Coral Hart move

The [ai-novel-factory](ai-novel-factory.md) model is layered authorship taken to its industrial extreme: Hart picks premises and supervises output at portfolio level; the LLM handles every other layer. The essay's framing — *"authors as showrunners of AI writers' rooms"* — makes the TV parallel do most of the legitimizing work. If we accept TV showrunners as authors of shows they didn't write word-by-word, why not a novelist with an AI writers' room? The essay leaves the question hanging.

## Open questions

- Will the resistance hierarchy stay stable, or will prose become acceptable the way background music did?
- In a disclosure regime, would readers want to know *which layers* were AI-assisted, not just whether AI was involved?
- Does Patterson precedent actually reduce AI resistance, or do audiences treat AI as categorically different?
