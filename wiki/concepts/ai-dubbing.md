# AI Dubbing

> **Translating a film into another language while altering the on-screen performer's face (lips, jaw) so the new audio matches the visual.** Distinct from plain audio dubbing because it modifies the image, not just the soundtrack. India is the leading test market.

## Why India

22 official languages and hundreds of dialects fragment the country into micro-markets — dubbing is essential for any film to become a national blockbuster. Audiences have long complained about mismatched lip movement in traditionally dubbed films, so **AI dubbing solves a pre-existing grievance rather than replacing live performance.** Lowest-friction AI-in-film play: expands reach, reduces friction, doesn't obviously displace craft jobs the way fully AI-generated content does.

## The technology claim

[neuralgarage](../entities/neuralgarage.md) co-founder Subhabrata Debnath: the technology preserves *"the performance, identity and the speaking style of the person"* while altering the face. In a Reuters demo, an AI-generated character speaking English was switched to German and, within minutes, appeared to speak fluent German with lips and jaw in sync. NeuralGarage's stack was used last year to dub Yash Raj Films' Hindi feature "War 2" into Telugu.

## Open questions

- How well does the approach generalize from AI characters (fully synthetic identity) to real actors with large fanbases?
- Does audience tolerance differ between dubbing real actors vs AI-generated performers?
- How do rights / consent frameworks handle facial alteration of real actors for localization?
