# AI Filmmaking in India

> **The first large market deploying AI across the full filmmaking stack — not just as a VFX aid, but as a substitute for substantial portions of the production pipeline.** The clearest natural experiment in AI-native commercial filmmaking, both because India produces more films than any country and because Hollywood-style union constraints are absent.

## Economic case

| Signal | Number |
|---|---|
| AI production cost vs traditional (mythology/fantasy) | **1/5** |
| AI timelines vs traditional | **1/4** |
| EY projection: revenue lift / cost reduction | ~10% / ~15% |
| Moviegoers 2019 → 2025 (Ormax) | 1.03B → 832M |

The cost claim is from [rahul-regulapati](../entities/rahul-regulapati.md) of [collective-artists-network](../entities/collective-artists-network.md)'s Galleri5. Backdrop: box-office concentration in a handful of hits post-pandemic; studios see AI as the efficiency lever that keeps production viable.

## The three plays

| Play | Mechanism | Lead operator |
|---|---|---|
| **Fully AI-generated** | [hindu-mythology-ai-genre](hindu-mythology-ai-genre.md) — eight-title deity slate; Mahabharat on [jiostar](../entities/jiostar.md) | [collective-artists-network](../entities/collective-artists-network.md) / Galleri5 |
| **[ai-dubbing](ai-dubbing.md)** | Cross India's 22-language market barrier with face-altering localization | [neuralgarage](../entities/neuralgarage.md) |
| **[ai-film-re-editing](ai-film-re-editing.md)** | Rewrite endings/scenes in catalog titles for re-release | [eros-media-world](../entities/eros-media-world.md) |

## Hollywood divergence

US studios are constrained by SAG-AFTRA (no digital alteration/replica without informed consent) and the DGA contract (no AI creative work without director consultation). Indian studios face no equivalent constraints. Dominic Lees (U. of Reading): *"If they can deliver, then the shift in AI filmmaking will be to India."*

## Production pipeline

Galleri5's hybrid template: actors wear mocap suits for body movement as 3D data; smartphones capture facial expressions; captured data drives AI-generated characters downstream. A bridge between classic mocap and emerging text-to-video tools.

## Substrate and partnerships

The text-to-video, image-to-image, and voice systems are all descendants of [diffusion-models](diffusion-models.md) / [score-based-generative-models](score-based-generative-models.md) (the [yang-song](../entities/yang-song.md) line). Indian studios are **net renters** of this substrate, owning pipelines, IP, and distribution around it — a textbook case of [own-your-substrate](../analyses/own-your-substrate.md).

| Partner | Commitment |
|---|---|
| [abundantia-entertainment](../entities/abundantia-entertainment.md) | $11M in-house AI studio; AI content → 1/3 of revenue in 3 years |
| Google (Veo 3, Flow) | 5-part cinematic series with Shakun Batra |
| Microsoft | Compute to Collective Artists Network |
| Nvidia | Shared stage at India's AI film fest, Feb 2026 |

## The reception paradox

Commercial and critical signals point opposite directions. Galleri5/JioStar Mahabharat: 26.5M+ views, 1.4/10 IMDb. Raanjhanaa AI re-release: 35% ticket share (12pts above average) despite lead-actor backlash. Anurag Kashyap's blunt framing: *"In India, cinema isn't about art. It's purely business, so studios are going to use it to make mythologicals. Our audience is a sucker for it."*

## Open questions

- Is tolerance specific to stylized genres (mythology/fantasy) or will it transfer to contemporary drama?
- Does the 1/5 cost claim hold outside mythology?
- Will Hollywood union constraints erode as Indian economics are demonstrated, or will the two industries permanently diverge?
