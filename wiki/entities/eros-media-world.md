# Eros Media World

> **Indian film studio pioneering AI re-editing of catalog titles.** Re-released *Raanjhanaa* (2013) with an AI-altered ending; now reviewing its full ~3,000-title catalog. The clearest working case for [ai-film-re-editing](../concepts/ai-film-re-editing.md).

## The Raanjhanaa re-release

The 2025 re-release replaced the protagonist's death with a happy reunion. Lead actor Dhanush said on X the remake had "stripped the film of its very soul."

Audience behavior contradicted the artistic backlash: PVR Inox told Reuters that **35% of available tickets sold for the Tamil version in August — 12 points above the 2025 average.** A clean instance of the reception paradox running through [ai-in-creative-writing](../concepts/ai-in-creative-writing.md) and [hindu-mythology-ai-genre](../concepts/hindu-mythology-ai-genre.md).

## The 3,000-title catalog review

Group CEO [pradeep-dwivedi](pradeep-dwivedi.md) told Reuters Eros is reviewing the full catalog "to identify candidates for AI-assisted adaptation," framed as "both a revenue opportunity and a creative renewal strategy." Partly defensive: Eros International's Indian unit saw operating revenue fall 44% last year.
