# Stefano Ermon

Stanford computer science professor and [yang-song](yang-song.md)'s PhD advisor. Co-author on every paper in Song's 2019–2022 sequence on [score-based-generative-models](../concepts/score-based-generative-models.md), including the original NCSN paper (NeurIPS 2019), the improved NCSNv2 (NeurIPS 2020), the ICLR 2021 SDE paper that won an Outstanding Paper Award and unified score-based modeling with [diffusion-models](../concepts/diffusion-models.md), the NeurIPS 2021 Spotlight on maximum-likelihood training, and the ICLR 2022 medical-imaging inverse-problems paper. His group at Stanford is one of the principal research centers for the score-based / diffusion model family.

Appears in this wiki only as a co-author of the [Yang Song blog post](../sources/2026-04-04-yang-song-score-based-generative-modeling.md) and the papers it synthesizes. Created as an entity page because he is substantively discussed in the post's historical narrative (not just a name-drop) and because the consistent advisor role across the entire paper sequence makes him a natural anchor for future sources in this theoretical lineage.
