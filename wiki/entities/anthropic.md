# Anthropic

> **AI safety company and Claude maker.** In this wiki on two threads: (1) a vertical life-sciences strategy via product + acqui-hire, and (2) a long-horizon agentic-coding methodology via the C compiler project and the Discovery team's CLAX walkthrough.

## Life sciences strategy

Building dedicated capability on two fronts:

| Front | Move |
|---|---|
| Product | [claude-for-life-sciences](claude-for-life-sciences.md), launched October 2025 |
| Talent + IP | April 2026 acqui-hire of [coefficient-bio](coefficient-bio.md), ~$400M all-stock, ~10 people from [prescient-design](prescient-design.md) lineage |

The combination suggests Anthropic wants first-party domain expertise — not just generic model access — shaping its vertical offerings.

## Long-horizon agentic coding

Two public demonstrations anchor the methodology (see [long-running-agentic-coding](../concepts/long-running-agentic-coding.md)):

| Demo | Artifact | Topology |
|---|---|---|
| [anthropic-c-compiler-project](anthropic-c-compiler-project.md) | Linux-kernel-capable C compiler | ~2,000 parallel sessions |
| [clax-project](clax-project.md) (via [siddharth-mishra-sharma](siddharth-mishra-sharma.md), **Discovery team**) | Differentiable JAX Boltzmann solver, sub-percent vs CLASS | Single sequential agent |

Together they make the point: long-horizon autonomous work is not one pattern — the **shape of the task** determines whether the right deployment is a swarm or a solo agent. The Discovery team appears to be Anthropic's internal group thinking about AI-assisted scientific research.
