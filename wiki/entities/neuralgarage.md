# NeuralGarage

Bengaluru-based AI startup providing dubbing technology to top Indian studios, including Yash Raj Films (Source: [[sources/2026-04-04-reuters-ai-indian-films]]).

## Technology

Co-founder Subhabrata Debnath demonstrated the system to Reuters: an AI-generated English-speaking character was given a German audio track and, within minutes, appeared to speak fluent German with lip and jaw in sync. The key claim is that the approach preserves "the performance, identity and the speaking style of the person" while altering the face enough to make the dubbing look natural — a bet that audiences will tolerate (or welcome) face alteration as long as the performer's identity is preserved.

## Notable use

NeuralGarage's technology was used last year to dub Yash Raj Films' Hindi feature "War 2" into Telugu for the South Indian market.

## Why it matters

Dubbing may be the lowest-friction entry point for AI in Indian film. India's 22 official languages and hundreds of dialects make dubbing a baseline requirement for any national blockbuster, and audiences have long complained about mismatched lip movement — so AI here solves a pre-existing grievance rather than replacing live performance.
