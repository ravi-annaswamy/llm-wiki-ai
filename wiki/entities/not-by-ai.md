# Not by AI

A certification-mark vendor offering human-made badges for websites, blogs, art, films, essays, books, and podcasts. One of the 12+ entrants in the [human-made-content-labelling](../concepts/human-made-content-labelling.md) landscape (Source: [2026-04-05-theverge-human-made-labels](../sources/2026-04-05-theverge-human-made-labels.md)).

## The 90% threshold

Not by AI's distinctive design choice is a **threshold rather than a purity test**: a creator may apply the badge if *at least 90 percent* of the work is made by a real human. This is a deliberate concession to the definition problem surfaced by quoted academics in the Verge piece — AI is now embedded enough in creative tooling that a 100% human standard would exclude almost all working creatives.

The weakness is that the claim is **voluntary and unverified**. The badge is a self-attestation, with no auditing of the 90% number and no mechanism for a third party to recompute it.

## Relevance

Not by AI is the clearest example of how the [human-made-content-labelling](../concepts/human-made-content-labelling.md) category is trying to handle the definition problem — by picking an arbitrary numeric threshold and trusting claimants. It bridges the purity of manual-audit schemes like [proudly-human](proudly-human.md) and the trust-based zero-verification of *Made by Human*.

The 90% threshold also foreshadows a possibly richer future design: per-layer claims (premise, outline, prose, revision), as the [layered-authorship](../concepts/layered-authorship.md) view of fiction suggests. Not by AI has not taken that step.
