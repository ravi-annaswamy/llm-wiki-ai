# Siddharth Mishra-Sharma

Researcher on Anthropic's **Discovery team**, with a background in cosmology. Author of the April 2026 Anthropic post describing how to apply long-running agentic coding workflows to scientific computing tasks (Source: [[sources/2026-04-04-anthropic-long-running-claude-scientific-computing]]).

In the post he walks through using Claude Opus 4.6 to build [[entities/clax-project]], a differentiable JAX reimplementation of a cosmological Boltzmann solver, benchmarked against the [[entities/class-boltzmann-solver]] C reference. He is explicit that the task is adjacent to — not inside — his core domain, which is part of the point: the experiment tests whether a non-expert can steer an agent further than domain experts have gone solo. He reports that following the agent's commit history turned out to be an effective way to osmose the science itself.

## Why he matters to the wiki

Mishra-Sharma is the first named practitioner the wiki has catalogued for the [[concepts/long-running-agentic-coding]] pattern applied to scientific code. His post is the reference document for several concept pages here: [[concepts/long-running-agentic-coding]], [[concepts/test-oracle-for-agents]], [[concepts/agent-persistent-memory]], [[concepts/ralph-loop]], [[concepts/agent-driven-scientific-computing]].

He works on the **Discovery team** at [[entities/anthropic]], which is positioned as the group thinking about AI-assisted scientific research and long-horizon autonomous work.
