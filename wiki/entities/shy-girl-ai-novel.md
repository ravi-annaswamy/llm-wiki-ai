# Shy Girl

> **The wiki's most prominent "AI writing unmasked" incident.** Horror novel credited to Mia Ballard; acquired by Hachette, then cancelled after [pangram](pangram.md) declared it 78% AI-generated — yet holds a 4/5 Amazon rating from hundreds of reviews written before the controversy.

## The timeline

| Step | Event |
|---|---|
| 1 | Self-published online under Mia Ballard's name; premise: young woman imprisoned as a "pet" by her sugar daddy |
| 2 | Found a substantial audience; 4/5 Amazon rating across hundreds of reviews |
| 3 | Acquired by **Hachette**, one of the Big Five US trade publishers |
| 4 | Readers noticed prose texture — *"endless adjectives and metaphors," "a chatbot's unvarying cadence"* |
| 5 | [pangram](pangram.md) analyzed the text: **78% AI-generated** |
| 6 | Ballard suggested a freelance editor may have run the manuscript through AI without her consent |
| 7 | Hachette cancelled publication |

## Why it matters

**The reception paradox.** The 4/5 rating was earned before disclosure. Reviewers praised the premise, not the prose — one explicitly acknowledged "the controversy" and still liked it: *"The premise sucked me in."* This is the same commercial-vs-critical decoupling documented for the Galleri5 / JioStar AI Mahabharat series (26.5M views, 1.4/10 IMDb — see [hindu-mythology-ai-genre](../concepts/hindu-mythology-ai-genre.md)). Two independent instances, two different media.

**Disclosure is the unresolved question.** Hachette's cancellation implies an industry judgment that the rating wouldn't survive mandatory disclosure. Central open question for [ai-in-creative-writing](../concepts/ai-in-creative-writing.md): is reader tolerance conditional on not knowing, or robust to it?

**Attribution fog.** The freelance-editor explanation — whether true or not — illustrates how AI collapses authorship norms. A traditional editor track-changes; an editor running a manuscript through an LLM produces something with no clear attribution boundary. [coral-hart](coral-hart.md) is the deliberate version of this collapse; Shy Girl is the accidental one.
