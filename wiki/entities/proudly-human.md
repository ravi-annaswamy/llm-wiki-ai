# Proudly Human

A certification-mark vendor that issues a "Proudly Human" label for published text, visual art, videography, and music. One of the broader-scope entrants in the fragmented [human-made-content-labelling](../concepts/human-made-content-labelling.md) landscape surveyed by The Verge (Source: [2026-04-05-theverge-human-made-labels](../sources/2026-04-05-theverge-human-made-labels.md)).

## Verification approach

Proudly Human relies on **manual audit** — creators submit process artifacts (sketches, drafts, working files) to a human reviewer. This is labor-intensive but is, per The Verge, "the most reliable method we currently have to establish if something was made by a real human."

## Enforcement

CEO **Trevor Woods** told The Verge that fraud prevention is not possible; enforcement is post-hoc legal action:

> "Like other certification marks and company logos, we cannot prevent fraudulently displaying the Proudly Human certification mark. However, we make it easy for consumers to verify it. If a bad actor identified by us refuses to stop using the label, we will take legal action against them."

Woods also noted the regulator gap: "Proudly Human has occasionally briefed government and industry associations but is not involved in formal negotiations regarding a unified human origin certification. The rapid evolution of AI capabilities and AI-generated content will outpace government and regulator responses."

## Relevance

Proudly Human is the clearest statement in the source of the **structural ceiling** any voluntary human-made labelling scheme hits: no regulatory backing, no prevention mechanism, only reactive legal enforcement. It illustrates why the landscape remains fragmented — each scheme faces the same constraints, none has a path to becoming the Fair Trade–equivalent unified authority.
